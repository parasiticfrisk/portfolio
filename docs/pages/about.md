I am a [GIS] and [restoration ecology] professional focused on ecological monitoring, [remote sensing], [biodiversity informatics], and [community science]. My work combines geospatial technologies, [field ecology], and environmental data [workflows] to support restoration, [conservation], and public engagement initiatives across [California].

My experience includes [geospatial analysis], GIS workflow development, [drone imagery] processing, remote sensing, ecological restoration, biodiversity monitoring, [urban forestry] inventories, participatory science workflows, environmental data management, and field data collection.

I have contributed to projects across riparian forests, wetlands, floodplains, grasslands, and urban ecosystems using [ArcGIS Pro], [ArcGIS Online], ArcGIS Field Maps, [GNSS] equipment, remote sensing workflows, and [Python]-based GIS automation.

I am particularly interested in restoration ecology, conservation technology, environmental GIS, [open source] geospatial tools, and community centered approaches to [environmental research] and [stewardship].

Beyond professional work, I actively participate in community science, [environmental education], scientific outreach, and [open knowledge] initiatives. I contribute to biodiversity [observations] and [species identifications] through [iNaturalist], support [collaborative] ecological research on [cynipid wasps] and [arthropods], and help develop accessible  workflows that improve public participation in science.
